Anaemia and its cup of Despair

DID YOU CONTRACT IT??

Anaemia is the most common blood condition in India, as many as 68.4% children and 66.4% women surveyed suffered from anaemia. Women, young children, and people with long-term diseases are more likely to have anaemia.

Read

Causes

There are more than 400 types of anaemia, and they’re divided into three groups: Anaemia caused by blood loss, Anaemia caused by decreased or faulty red blood cell production, Anaemia caused by destruction of red blood cells

Symptoms

The signs of anaemia can be so mild that you might not even notice them. At a certain point, as your blood cells decrease, symptoms often develop like: Fast or unusual heartbeat, Headache, Problems with growth, for children and teens, Skin that’s pale or yellow

Treatment

Your treatment will depend on your type of anaemia. There are lot of causes, so there are also many treatments available. Including various home remedies but we suggest to go to a professional for routine check-up.

Bringing light to the problem

Anaemia in children and women worsened in the past half-a-decade across most states and Union territories,
according to the latest National Family Health Survey (NFHS).
And to put it simply anaemia If left untreated, iron-deficiency anaemia can cause serious health problems. Having too little oxygen in the body
can damage organs. With anaemia, the heart must work harder to make up for the lack of red blood cells or haemoglobin. This extra work can harm the heart.
